Biography

Clément Parisot is a computer engineer graduated from TELECOM Nancy (France) and UQAC (Canada) in 2014. He works as a system and network administrator. He worked 2 years in the technical team of Grid’5000 in Inria Nancy, installing and configuring new clusters and contributing to Kwapi, an energy and network monitoring tool. His actual work consists of supporting and building a big scale HPC platform in Belval (Luxembourg) as a member of the UL HPC Team in University of Luxembourg since february 2017.

He has a good experience in Linux systems (Debian, Centos, RedHat…). His hobbies are 3D printing (MicroDelta Rework), photography and robotics as a member of the ESIAL Robotik association. He also organised several time as a volounteer the Mondial Air Balloons, worldwide hot air balloon meeting, in the communication/video team.
